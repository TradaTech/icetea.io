FAQ

  • What is Icetea?

    Icetea is a smart-contract-enabled blockchain with a focus on smooth adoption for both users and developers. It attempts to bring 'blockchain' to regular people.

  • Who makes it?

    It is developed and supported by TradaTech team. The software is open-source and outside contributors are welcomed.

  • Why do you make it?

    In our opinion, it is better for the society as a whole if everyone has the tools to conveniently control their own physical and spiritual assets. Then, they won't have to delegate that task to others, who often abuse their trust.

    We realize that blockchain technology has the potential to turn that vision into reality. Yet the 'convenient' part is missing.

    It is like this: you invite people to come and live in your area, because there is a lot of green trees and fresh air there. Great, isn't it? But it is too far from their workplaces, no good transportation, no school nearby for their kids, no entertainment, etc.

    Icetea brings the 'convenient' part into the picture. For both users and developers.

  • How do you do it?

    Some examples.

    Icetea distinguishes between regular app accounts (like your social network account) with bank accounts (i.e. accounts which store, receive, and transfer coins and tokens). By doing that, it is possible for wallets to present users with appropriate confirmation for each type of account. For example, for regular accounts, users have the option to trust the dapp and tell the wallet not to ask again. That reduces much friction, yeah, get rid of those i-don't-care MetaMask dialogs.

    Icetea allows a smart contract to pay fees for its users. That open up room for free, freemium, and free-trial dapps. Users don't need to buy some 'coins' first just to try the app. In addition, they could pay by traditional methods like credit card, and don't need to directly buy any 'tokens' at all if the dapp is designed so.

    Icetea deals with 'accounts', not 'address'. There's no differentiation between a 'native coin' and a 'token'. You can delegate to others to use your account. Multisig, 2-factor, lost key, and property inheritance are then built-in and intuitive.

    If you are a dapp developer, you could use most of the abundance of existing NodeJS modules to author your own smart contracts. Just npm install or yarn it. It is that simple. There are more to learn though, like utilizing WebAssembly for performance-critical spots, or writing decentralized bots. There is a decentralized gate for querying external datasources.

    We are also researching about 'autonomous zone' - think of it as a sidechain but you don't have to setup 'miners/validators/master nodes'. Its security is provided by mother chain. This gives dapp developers uttermost freedom ever.

    There is more to say. We will publish docs on this gradually.
